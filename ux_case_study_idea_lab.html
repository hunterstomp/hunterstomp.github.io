Idea Lab Measurement Framework

Project Summary

Client: Internal Innovation Division

Role: UX Strategist, Framework Architect

Focus: Create a repeatable methodology to evaluate and prioritize UX innovation experiments across T-Mobile’s Idea Lab initiatives

Problem

Teams lacked a consistent way to measure success across initiatives. Without a shared system for risk, ROI, or effort trade-offs, prioritization often came down to intuition or politics.

Goal

Design a UX-informed decision system for hypothesis testing, prioritization, and stakeholder buy-in that encourages rapid iteration and transparent value scoring.

Research & Framework Creation

  • Studied SAFe’s Weighted Shortest Job First (WSJF) principles【14†Idea Lab_Measurements Framework】
  • Mapped decision gates to UX phases: Intake → Rank → Build → Validate → Commercialize
  • Created role-based templates for core teams, VPs, and steering committees
“What gets prioritized? The initiative with the most benefit and the least amount of work.”

UX Approach

  • Translated complex KPIs into visual cards with benchmark references
  • Designed inputs for subjective scores that convert to objective values
  • Paired qualitative tools (interviews, testing) with quantitative insights (lift tests, ROI)

Outcomes

  • Cross-team visibility into ranking criteria
  • Unified testing process across marketing, care, and digital product teams
  • Improved time-to-decision for test investments and go/no-go gates

Key UX Wins

  • 📊 Experience-agnostic templates that still work across device channels
  • 🎯 KPI scoring logic grounded in UX thinking, not just finance
  • ♻️ Created space for loops: stop, edit and re-test vs. forced success

Quotes

“Helps remove subjectivity when ranking ideas. This feels fair.”
“Now we can finally say why something wins without people fighting.”

What I’d Improve

  • Build a self-service dashboard for teams to explore their own idea scoring
  • Introduce gamification or badges to reward evidence-based proposals
  • Layer in AI-generated scorecards for live pitch environments
